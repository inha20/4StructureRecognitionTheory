# Concept as Lens Hypothesis

Status: Exploratory Hypothesis

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Question

Are concepts merely labels, or do they function as lenses that enable new observations?

---

## Motivation

Many research advances appear to occur after the introduction of a new concept.

Examples include:

- Symmetry
- Invariance
- Evolution
- Entropy
- Algorithm

Once learned, these concepts often enable observers to perceive structures that were previously unnoticed.

---

## Working Hypothesis

Concepts are not merely descriptions.

Concepts may function as cognitive lenses that alter what an observer can perceive.

---

## Proposed Cycle

Phenomenon
→ Concept
→ Expanded Perception
→ New Observation
→ New Question
→ New Research
→ New Concept

---

## Examples

Symmetry enables recognition of structural similarities.

Invariance enables recognition of preserved properties under transformation.

Structure discovery enables recognition of relationships among observations.

---

## Relationship to Structure Recognition Theory

Structure Recognition Theory may itself function as a conceptual lens.

If successful, the theory should help observers identify new structures, questions, and research opportunities.

---

## Open Questions

- What makes a concept valuable?
- Are some concepts more generative than others?
- Can concepts be evaluated by the new questions they generate?
- How do concepts change perception?

---

## Notes

This document records a working hypothesis rather than a conclusion.

Future revisions are expected.
