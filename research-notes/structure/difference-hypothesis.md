# Difference Hypothesis

Status: Exploratory Hypothesis

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Question

Why do some differences attract attention while others do not?

This question emerged during the development of the Research Generation Model.

---

## Observation

The world contains an enormous number of differences.

Most are ignored.

Only a small subset become:

- Noticeable
- Interesting
- Meaningful
- Worth investigating

This suggests that not all differences are cognitively equivalent.

---

## Proposed Model

Difference
→ Attention
→ Structure
→ Meaning
→ Question
→ Research

---

## Candidate Attention-Capturing Differences

Examples may include:

- Symmetry
- Symmetry breaking
- Repetition
- Contrast
- Anomaly
- Unexpected regularity
- Invariance
- Rare events
- Violations of expectation

---

## Difference and Structure

Not every difference produces structure.

However, some differences reveal relationships.

When relationships become visible, structure may emerge.

---

## Difference and Meaning

A difference may become meaningful when it appears:

- Non-random
- Explanatory
- Generalizable
- Connected to other phenomena

---

## Relationship to Structure Recognition Theory

Difference may be the earliest stage in the research-generation process.

Difference
→ Attention
→ Structure
→ Meaning
→ Question
→ Research
