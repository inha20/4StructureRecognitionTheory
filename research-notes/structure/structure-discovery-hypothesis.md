# Structure Discovery Hypothesis

Status: Exploratory Hypothesis

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Question

Why do humans discover structure?

This question is one of the central questions of Structure Recognition Theory.

---

## Observation

Humans often appear to notice more than individual facts.

Instead, humans frequently perceive:

- Relationships
- Regularities
- Symmetries
- Invariances
- Correspondences

These observations suggest that human cognition may be naturally oriented toward structure discovery.

---

## Working Definition

Structure discovery refers to the recognition of relationships that connect multiple observations into a coherent whole.

Examples include:

- Multiplication-table patterns
- Euler characteristic
- Pascal's triangle relationships
- Symmetric Boolean functions
- Karnaugh-map structures

---

Pattern
→ Structure Recognition

## Candidate Structural Features

Structures that humans frequently notice may include:

- Symmetry
- Repetition
- Invariance
- Correspondence
- Hierarchy
- Contrast
- Anomaly

---

## Relationship to Research

Many research questions appear to emerge after structure discovery.

Examples:

- Why does this symmetry exist?
- Why does this pattern repeat?
- Why does this structure remain invariant?

This suggests that structure discovery may often precede research.
