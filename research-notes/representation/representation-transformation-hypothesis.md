# Representation Transformation Hypothesis

Status: Exploratory Hypothesis

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Claim

Many difficult problems become easier not because computation is reduced, but because the representation of the problem is transformed into a form where structure becomes visible.

This is currently a hypothesis rather than a conclusion.

---

## Motivation

Humans often struggle with large amounts of symbolic information.

However, the same underlying problem may become significantly easier when represented in a different form.

This suggests that problem difficulty may depend not only on the problem itself but also on its representation.

---

## Hypothesis

Representation transformation can amplify human structure-discovery ability.

A successful representation does not necessarily reduce information.

Instead, it makes important structures easier to perceive.

---

## Examples

### Karnaugh Maps

Transformation:

Boolean Expression
→ Karnaugh Map

Result:

- Adjacency becomes visible
- Simplification becomes visual
- Symmetry becomes observable
- Structural patterns become recognizable

### Data Visualization

Transformation:

Raw Data
→ Visual Representation

Result:

- Trends become visible
- Outliers become visible
- Clusters become visible

### Foldit

Transformation:

Protein Folding Problem
→ Interactive Spatial Puzzle

Result:

- Human spatial intuition becomes useful
- Structural relationships become easier to explore

### Mathematical Graphs

Transformation:

Equation
→ Graph

Result:

- Global behavior becomes visible
- Symmetry becomes visible
- Relationships become easier to interpret

## Working Model

Problem
→ Representation Transformation
→ Structure Visibility
→ Structure Discovery
→ Insight

---

## Relationship to Structure Recognition Theory

This hypothesis suggests that structure discovery is influenced by the representations available to the observer.

Different representations may reveal different structures even when the underlying information remains unchanged.
