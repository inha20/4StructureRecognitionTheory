# Human-AI Collaboration Model

Status: Exploratory Model

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Question

How should humans and AI collaborate in the process of discovery and research?

---

## Motivation

Recent AI systems have become increasingly capable of:

- Computation
- Search
- Summarization
- Organization
- Visualization

At the same time, humans continue to play an important role in identifying meaningful phenomena and generating questions.

---

## Proposed Division of Roles

Human:

- Attention
- Structure discovery
- Meaning attribution
- Question generation
- Interpretation

AI:

- Computation
- Information retrieval
- Organization
- Summarization
- Visualization

---

## Collaboration Cycle

Phenomenon
→ Human Attention
→ Structure Discovery
→ Question Generation
→ AI-Assisted Exploration
→ New Insights
→ New Structures

---

## Example: Karnaugh Map Research

Human contributions:

- Notice checkerboard patterns
- Recognize symmetry
- Ask why patterns occur

AI contributions:

- Generate explanations
- Connect concepts
- Organize hypotheses
- Produce documentation

---

## Structure Discoverer Hypothesis Connection

This model is compatible with the hypothesis that human comparative advantage may increasingly lie in structure discovery.

AI may increasingly serve as a computational and organizational partner.

---

## Open Questions

- Can AI independently discover meaningful structures?
- Can AI generate genuinely original research questions?
- Which tasks should remain human-centered?
- How should education prepare students for collaborative intelligence?

---

This model emerged from ongoing discussions involving Structure Recognition Theory, research generation, and AI-assisted exploration.
