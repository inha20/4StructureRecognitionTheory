# Research Worthiness Hypothesis

Status: Exploratory Hypothesis

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Question

Why do some phenomena appear worth investigating?

This question emerged from the development of Structure Recognition Theory.

---

## Observation

Humans encounter countless patterns and events.

Only a small fraction become research questions.

This suggests the existence of a selection process between observation and research.

---

## Proposed Model

Difference
→ Attention
→ Structure
→ Meaning
→ Research Worthiness
→ Question
→ Research

---

## Candidate Sources of Research Worthiness

A phenomenon may appear worth investigating when it is perceived as:

- Non-random
- Surprising
- Explanatory
- Generalizable
- Deeply connected to other phenomena
- Potentially revealing hidden structure

---

## Relationship to Meaning

Meaning and research worthiness are related but distinct.

A phenomenon may be meaningful without becoming a research topic.

Research worthiness emerges when meaning motivates investigation.

---

## Relationship to Question Generation

Research worthiness may act as a bridge between meaning and question generation.

Meaning
→ Research Worthiness
→ Question
