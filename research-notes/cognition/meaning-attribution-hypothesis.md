# Meaning Attribution Hypothesis

Status: Exploratory Hypothesis

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Question

Why do some structures appear meaningful?

Structure recognition alone does not necessarily generate research.

Many structures are observed but quickly forgotten.

Others become meaningful, memorable, and worthy of investigation.

This suggests the existence of a process of meaning attribution.

---

## Observation

Humans frequently encounter patterns.

Examples include:

- Repetition
- Symmetry
- Correlation
- Regularity

However, only a subset of these patterns appear significant.

The same pattern may appear meaningful to one observer and insignificant to another.

---

## Working Hypothesis

Meaning attribution is a cognitive process that assigns significance to perceived structures.

Proposed process:

Pattern
→ Attention
→ Structure
→ Meaning

Meaning attribution may occur when a structure appears:

- Non-random
- Elegant
- Explanatory
- Surprising
- Connected to other phenomena

---

## Meaning and Non-Randomness

One possible source of meaning is perceived non-randomness.

When a structure appears unlikely to be accidental, humans may become motivated to explain it.

Examples:

- Mathematical identities
- Symmetry
- Repeated patterns
- Structural invariants

---

## Meaning and Research

Research may not begin with structure recognition alone.

A second step may occur:

Structure
→ Meaning
→ Question

Meaning attribution may therefore act as a bridge between observation and research.

---

## Relationship to Structure Recognition Theory

Structure Recognition Theory seeks to explain how humans come to see something new.

Meaning attribution may represent a critical stage between structure discovery and question generation.

---

## Open Questions

- Why do different people assign meaning differently?
- Can meaning attribution be measured?
- Is meaning attribution learned or innate?
- Can AI perform meaning attribution?
