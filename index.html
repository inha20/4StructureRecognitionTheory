Structure Recognition Theory

How do humans detect explanatory significance?

The central question of the program: How do humans come to see something new?

Abstract

Structure Recognition Theory (SRT) is a theoretical framework investigating the cognitive mechanisms by which humans discover structures, assign meaning to them, and generate research questions. The theory proposes that research does not begin from structure detection alone, but from detecting explanatory significance — a sense that a structure warrants investigation.

Built on three empirical case studies (Papers 1–3 on Karnaugh map patterns, symmetric Boolean functions, and variable rearrangement invariance), SRT currently comprises ten exploratory hypotheses (H1–H10), formal definitions of Structure, Attention, and Explanatory Significance, and a hierarchy of fifteen research questions (Q1–Q15 across eight levels).
