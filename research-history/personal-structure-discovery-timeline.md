# Personal Structure Discovery Timeline

Status: Research History

## Purpose

This document records major moments in which structure, pattern, symmetry, invariance, or meaningful relationships became visible.

The timeline is intended as a historical record of observations that eventually contributed to the development of Structure Recognition Theory.

---

## Elementary School

### Multiplication Table

Observation:

Repeated addition generates recurring numerical structures.

Different multiplication tables exhibit recognizable patterns, correspondences, and regularities.

---

## Middle School

### Algebraic Expansion Identities

Observation:

Algebraic identities can be understood geometrically through rectangular area models.

Tabular expansion reveals structural relationships among terms.

### Euler Characteristic

Observation:

For many polyhedra:

V − E + F = 2

This was an early encounter with invariance.

Different shapes appeared to share a common underlying structure.

---

## High School

### Reading Comprehension and Logical Structure

Observation:

Complex texts can be understood through relationships among ideas rather than individual sentences.

### Function Construction Problems

Observation:

Function equations emerge from interactions among multiple conditions.

The solution often reflects an underlying structure rather than isolated facts.

### Genetics and Pascal's Triangle

Observation:

Patterns of inheritance appear connected to combinatorial structures.

Independent assortment naturally generates binomial coefficients.

### Symmetric Boolean Functions

Observation:

Some Boolean functions remain unchanged under variable exchange.

Symmetry itself becomes an object of study.

### Variable Rearrangement

Observation:

Structural patterns may remain recognizable even after changing variable order.

This suggested the existence of deeper structural invariance.

---

## University

### Karnaugh Maps

Observation:

Boolean expressions can be transformed into visual structures.

Research topics emerged including:

- Checkerboard patterns
- Symmetry
- Structural invariance
- Visual pattern recognition
- Variable rearrangement
