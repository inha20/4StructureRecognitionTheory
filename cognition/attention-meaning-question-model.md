# Attention, Meaning, and Question Generation Model

Status: Exploratory Hypothesis

Date: 2026-06-03

---

## Central Question

Why do some phenomena attract attention while others are ignored?

---

## Motivation

Human beings are constantly surrounded by patterns.

However, only a small subset of these patterns become noticeable, meaningful, and worth investigating.

---

## Proposed Model

Pattern
→ Attention
→ Structure
→ Meaning
→ Question
→ Research

---

## Attention

Possible attention-capturing features include:

- Symmetry
- Repetition
- Contrast
- Invariance
- Anomaly
- Unexpected regularity

---

## Structure

After attention is directed toward a phenomenon, structure may become visible.

Examples include multiplication tables, Pascal's triangle, symmetric Boolean functions, and Karnaugh-map patterns.

---

## Meaning

The observer may begin to perceive non-randomness, significance, or explanatory potential.

---

## Question Generation

Meaning often leads to questions such as:

- Why does this pattern exist?
- Why does this symmetry appear?
- Why does the structure remain unchanged?

---

## Research

Research begins when questions become sufficiently important to investigate.

---

## Relationship to Structure Recognition Theory

Structure discovery may be one component of a broader process involving attention, meaning attribution, and question generation.

---

## Open Questions

- Why do certain structures attract attention?
- Why are some structures perceived as meaningful?
- Is meaning necessary for question generation?
- Can AI experience an analogous process?
- Can education intentionally develop these abilities?
