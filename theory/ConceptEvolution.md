# Concept Evolution

## Motivation

Structure Recognition Theory initially focused on how humans discover structures and transform them into research questions.

However, research progress appears to involve another process:

How do concepts enable the discovery of new structures?

---

## Core Observation

Many important discoveries seem to occur after a new concept becomes available.

Examples:

- XOR reveals checkerboard-like Boolean patterns.
- Invariance reveals similarities across transformed representations.
- Symmetry reveals relationships between apparently different structures.

A concept may function as a lens rather than merely a label.

---

## Concept as Lens Hypothesis

Phenomenon
    ↓
Concept
    ↓
New Observation
    ↓
New Question
    ↓
Research
    ↓
New Concept

Concepts do not merely explain existing observations.

They may also make previously invisible observations visible.

---

## What Makes a Concept Good?

Candidate properties:

- Compression
    - Explains many phenomena using a small set of ideas.

- Generativity
    - Produces new questions.

- Predictive Power
    - Suggests observations before they are made.

- Transferability
    - Applies across multiple domains.

- Visibility Enhancement
    - Makes hidden structures easier to perceive.

---

## Open Questions

- Are concepts discovered or invented?
- Why do some concepts generate more research than others?
- Can concept evolution itself be studied systematically?
- Is scientific progress driven primarily by observations or by concepts?
